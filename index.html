Our Philosophy

The Minimal
Effective Dose

Good psychiatry is not about adding more. It is about finding the least that truly helps — and having the discipline to stop there.

So much of modern medicine drifts toward complexity: another prescription, another adjustment, another bottle on the shelf. Walden Mental Health is built on the opposite instinct. We prescribe with restraint, toward the smallest dose and the fewest medications that let you feel like yourself again.

That means we are just as willing to simplify a regimen as to start one — to review what you are taking, question what no longer serves you, and clear away the clutter so the things that matter can work. Fewer moving parts. Clearer outcomes. A life that feels like yours.

01

Start low, go slow

The smallest effective dose, reached deliberately — not the fastest one.

02

Subtract, don't just add

Deprescribing is treatment. We review and remove what no longer earns its place.

03

Treat the person

Medication is one tool among many — never the whole plan.

Why Simplicity

Less, Done Well

Simplicity isn't doing less for the sake of it. It's the harder, more careful work of finding what actually helps — and protecting you from everything that doesn't.

◐

Fewer Medications

Every prescription has to earn its place. We favor one well-chosen medication over a stack of partial fixes, and we watch for the side effects complexity hides.

⟲

Thoughtful Deprescribing

If you arrive on more than you need, we'll carefully and safely taper toward less — a plan to simplify, not just to maintain.

❖

Whole-Person Care

Sleep, stress, movement, meaning. We treat the conditions a life is lived in, so medication can do its job and no more.
